About Us

Established in 2019, the Georgian Biotechnology Association (GeBA) is a nonprofit, non-governmental organization dedicated to advancing biotechnology, applied biosciences, green solutions, and innovation in Georgia. GeBA unites around 200 members, including young researchers, professionals, and businesses working across interdisciplinary fields such as agriculture, food, environment, and health. The association serves as a key bridge between research, innovation, education, business, and society. GeBA is represented by experienced researchers and experts with extensive backgrounds in sustainable business development and consulting.

Our Activities

  • GeBA partners with leading Georgian universities and collaborates with higher education and research institutions across the Eastern Partnership region. Through its network of research institutions and businesses, GeBA provides strong regional capacity for testing, demonstrating, and scaling innovative biotechnological, agri-food, climate-smart, environmentally sustainable, and health-related solutions. The association actively supports stakeholder engagement, dissemination, capacity building, and business model development, effectively translating research results into real-life applications and facilitating market uptake in Georgia and across the wider Eastern Partnership region, including Armenia, Moldova, and Ukraine. GeBA Expertise • Climate-Smart and Sustainable Agriculture: On-farm trials, large-scale demonstrations, and living labs integrating low-emission and circular economy practices. • Agri-Food Innovation & Postharvest Solutions: Pilot projects improving productivity, food quality, and safety. • Phytosanitary Assessment & Plant Health: Implementation of plant health monitoring, pest management, and biosecurity measures. • Economic Analysis & Market Uptake: Cost–benefit analysis, economic modeling, and feasibility studies to support business decision-making and commercialization. • Environmental Solutions: Resource-efficient technologies, waste reduction strategies, and bio-based green innovations. • Biotechnology and Advanced Health Solutions • Healthy Food Systems and Nutrition • Disease prevention, health promotion, and well-being throughout life. • Environmental and climate-related health risks. • Improve prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of major diseases. • Capacity Building & Knowledge Transfer: Training, technical assistance, and facilitation of research-industry linkages across the region.
