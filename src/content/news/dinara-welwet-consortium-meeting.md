We are pleased to announce that the first online consortium meeting of the Erasmus+ Youth project DINARA-WELWET - “Strengthening Youth Capacities in Wildlife Conservation Through One Health Initiatives and Virtual Reality Technology” was successfully held on February 17.

During the meeting, project partners introduced their teams, revisited the core objectives of the initiative, and discussed the key milestones and expected outcomes. Special attention was given to strengthening youth engagement in wildlife conservation through the innovative integration of the One Health approach and virtual reality technologies.

The consortium also outlined the next implementation steps and coordinated responsibilities to ensure effective project progress in the coming months.

As part of the upcoming activities, National Workshops are scheduled to take place in March. These workshops will play a crucial role in engaging young participants, sharing knowledge, and building practical capacities at the national level.

Further details regarding the workshop agendas, participating organisations, and speakers will be shared soon - stay tuned!

We look forward to an exciting and impactful collaboration ahead. 🌱✨
