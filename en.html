About Us

Established in 2019, the Georgian Biotechnology Association (GeBA) is a nonprofit, non-governmental organization dedicated to advancing biotechnology, applied biosciences, green solutions, and innovation in Georgia. GeBA unites around 200 members, including young researchers, professionals, and businesses working across interdisciplinary fields such as agriculture, food, environment, and health. The association serves as a key bridge between research, innovation, education, business, and society. GeBA is represented by experienced researchers and experts with extensive backgrounds in sustainable business development and consulting.

Activities

The association has implemented and partnered with projects funded by the EU, USAID, Tbilisi State University, and the National Science Foundation of Georgia. It has facilitated exchange programs with European universities, conducted a series of research conferences, and actively provided training and consulting services to farmers, agribusinesses, food processors, diagnostic and testing laboratories, and various businesses. The association actively supports participation in international research frameworks, including Horizon Europe and COST actions, fostering collaboration and innovation at both national and European levels.
